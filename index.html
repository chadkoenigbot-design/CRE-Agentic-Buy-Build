The Core Argument

The old binary was already wrong. AI makes the cost of getting it wrong catastrophic.

For decades, corporate real estate organizations wrestled with a single question: do we buy technology off the shelf, or invest in building it ourselves? The answer usually came down to budget cycles and vendor relationships.

AI has shattered that simplicity. Vendors are wrapping AI capabilities around legacy platforms faster than buyers can evaluate them. Build projects require AI expertise that competes with Silicon Valley for talent. And the velocity of change means a decision made in Q1 can be obsolete by Q3.

The leaders who are winning aren't asking "buy or build?" — they're asking "which of the five paths fits our actual situation right now?"

The Velocity Problem

Foundation models improve on 6–12 month cycles. Decisions anchored to today's capabilities age badly. Static vendor evaluations are already obsolete on signing day.

🏛️

The Moat Illusion

CRE data is increasingly a commodity. The competitive advantage isn't owning proprietary data — it's the operational intelligence built on top of it. That changes the build calculus entirely.

🧩

The Integration Tax

Most enterprise CRE stacks weren't designed for AI. Adding AI capability to brittle integrations multiplies technical debt faster than it creates value.

⚖️

The Talent Equation

Building in-house demands ML engineers, data scientists, and CRE domain experts simultaneously. That intersection is vanishingly rare and extraordinarily expensive.

Strategic patience

🛒 Buy — Adopt the Market Standard

Best When

  • The problem is solved by existing software
  • Your use case is non-differentiating
  • Speed to market outweighs customization
  • Your team lacks implementation capacity
  • Total cost of ownership favors SaaS

Watch Out For

  • Vendor lock-in at inflection points
  • AI features bolted on, not native
  • Pricing models that punish scale
  • Implementation complexity disguised as simplicity

CRE Examples

  • Lease abstraction AI tools
  • Market data / benchmarking platforms
  • Standard portfolio analytics
  • FM workflow automation

⚙️ Configure — Buy the Engine, Own the Tuning

Best When

  • Strong platform exists but doesn't fit your processes out-of-box
  • Your data model is complex or proprietary
  • You have internal technical capacity
  • Vendor has strong extensibility / API ecosystem

Watch Out For

  • Configuration creep becoming a shadow build
  • Version upgrade conflicts destroying customizations
  • Hidden professional services costs
  • Over-engineering non-critical workflows

CRE Examples

  • IWMS platform configuration for portfolio complexity
  • Space utilization dashboards on sensor platforms
  • Transaction management workflow customization

🔗 Augment — Own the Intelligence, Rent the Rails

Best When

  • You have valuable proprietary data
  • Existing platforms lack native AI for your use case
  • You can access AI APIs / foundation models
  • The differentiation is in interpretation, not plumbing

Watch Out For

  • API dependency creating new vendor risk
  • Data pipeline fragility
  • Model drift without monitoring
  • Internal capability to maintain the AI layer

CRE Examples

  • AI deal scoring on top of CRM data
  • Portfolio optimization layer on IWMS
  • Occupancy prediction models on sensor feeds
  • LLM-powered lease Q&A on document stores

🏗️ Build — Proprietary for Competitive Advantage

Best When

  • The capability is a genuine competitive differentiator
  • No vendor can solve it adequately
  • You have (or can hire) the talent
  • Long-term ownership economics are superior
  • IP protection is strategically critical

Watch Out For

  • Underestimating maintenance burden
  • Confusing "different" with "proprietary advantage"
  • Talent risk after launch
  • Opportunity cost of engineering distraction
  • The moment vendors catch up

CRE Examples

  • Proprietary site selection models
  • Internal carbon / ESG analytics platforms
  • Custom portfolio scenario modeling
  • Broker-facing client intelligence tools

⏳ Wait — Strategic Patience Over Premature Commitment

Best When

  • Market is nascent and vendor consolidation is imminent
  • Your data isn't ready for AI
  • Organizational change capacity is already maxed
  • The problem isn't urgent enough to absorb risk

Watch Out For

  • Wait becoming permanent inaction
  • Losing ground to peers who move first
  • Confusing uncertainty with infeasibility
  • Using "wait" to avoid a hard internal decision

How to Wait Well

  • Define the trigger conditions that end the wait
  • Invest in data foundation in the meantime
  • Run controlled pilots to reduce future uncertainty
  • Assign an owner to monitor the space
